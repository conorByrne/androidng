CRIBS

Crib (even if you plan to co-sleep at first you may want to have a crib set up for naps and later), Bassinet, cradle or co-sleeper

• In addition to finding a crib that meets current safety standards, look for a solid wood crib treated with a non-toxic finish.

• If you do purchase furniture made in non-natural materials, or if your furniture is treated with a finish or wood sealer that contains chemicals, place the furniture outside or in a ventilated garage for at least a month before you use it so that some of the fumes can off-gas.

• Used furniture more than five years old has most likely off-gassed any harmful chemicals, which makes buying or borrowing used furniture an excellent way to be health conscious, stay within a small budget, and practice recycling.

However, some hand-me-down cribs may not meet current safety standards and older cribs made before 1978 might contain lead paint.

For more information about cribs: National Safety Council
